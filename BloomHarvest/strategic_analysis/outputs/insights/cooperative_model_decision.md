## Strategic Decision Framework

### **OPTION A: Launch Track 1 FIRST (Solidarity Circle)**

**Timeline**: 3-month pilot → 3-month reflection → Track 2 launch at Month 9

**Advantages**:
1. ✅ **Lower barrier to entry**: $0-25/month = easy yes, test governance without financial risk
2. ✅ **Aligned with SAREC insights**: Nada's "fitra over well-being", Hany's "focus on people not projects"
3. ✅ **Build trust capital**: Demonstrate long-term commitment before asking for revenue share
4. ✅ **Fresh relationships**: ACSI3/4 teams = recent Bloom engagement (warm contacts)
5. ✅ **Test cooperative governance**: Co-design Arabic-first model, learn what works
6. ✅ **Natural pipeline**: Solidarity → Investment (entrepreneurs "graduate" as they scale)
7. ✅ **Crisis-responsive**: Can support Gaza/war-affected entrepreneurs immediately (Salah/Salam paused cohort)

**Disadvantages**:
1. ⚠️ **No revenue**: $0 income, requires external funding or Bilal's time donation
2. ⚠️ **Sustainability unclear**: How long can solidarity model run without income?
3. ⚠️ **May not lead to Track 2**: Pre-revenue entrepreneurs may never reach investment stage

**Budget required**: $5,000 for 3-month pilot (8-10 entrepreneurs)

**Risk mitigation**:
- Secure $5K grant or use personal funds for pilot
- Design Track 1 as "values alignment filter" → only successful solidarity members invited to Track 2
- Set 6-month checkpoint: If solidarity works, launch Track 2 at Month 9

---

### **OPTION B: Launch Track 2 FIRST (Investment Cooperative)**

**Timeline**: 6-month investment pilot → Use revenue to fund Track 1 at Month 12

**Advantages**:
1. ✅ **Revenue generation**: 2-5% share = recurring income to fund solidarity circle
2. ✅ **Stronger foundation**: Financial stake = more committed entrepreneurs
3. ✅ **Proven model**: Revenue-share cooperatives exist (precedent, templates)
4. ✅ **Higher impact**: $60K capital pool = significant support to 6 entrepreneurs
5. ✅ **Track 2 funds Track 1**: Use investment revenue to subsidize solidarity circles (cross-subsidy model)

**Disadvantages**:
1. ⚠️ **Cold relationships**: Top LGA1 teams = 6 years since program, need warm outreach
2. ⚠️ **Higher risk**: $80K budget required upfront, 10-year break-even
3. ⚠️ **Complexity**: Legal contracts, revenue tracking, due diligence = overhead
4. ⚠️ **No stage data**: Most teams missing stage info, revenue verification needed
5. ⚠️ **Extractive optics**: 2-5% revenue share may feel like "tax" vs solidarity

**Budget required**: $80,000 for 6-month pilot (6 entrepreneurs)

**Risk mitigation**:
- Start smaller: 3 entrepreneurs, $30K capital pool = test before scaling
- Warm outreach: Reference specific Bloom program memory, offer value-first (free consultation)
- Due diligence phase: Verify revenue before capital deployment
- Frame as "cooperative ownership" not "investment return" (values alignment)

---

### **OPTION C: Launch BOTH in PARALLEL**

**Timeline**: Simultaneous 6-month pilots for both tracks

**Advantages**:
1. ✅ **Serve different needs**: Track 1 = values/wellbeing, Track 2 = capital/scale
2. ✅ **Test both models**: Learn which resonates more, iterate based on data
3. ✅ **Cross-pollination**: Solidarity entrepreneurs can see "graduates" in Track 2, aspire to scale
4. ✅ **Diversified risk**: If one track fails, other may succeed
5. ✅ **Faster learning**: 6 months of parallel data vs 12 months sequential

**Disadvantages**:
1. ⚠️ **Resource constraints**: $85K total budget + double the coordination time
2. ⚠️ **Diluted focus**: Managing two distinct models simultaneously = complexity
3. ⚠️ **Confusing brand**: "What is Bloom Cooperative?" = hard to explain
4. ⚠️ **Quality risk**: Spreading thin may mean neither track gets sufficient attention

**Budget required**: $85,000 ($5K Track 1 + $80K Track 2)

**Risk mitigation**:
- Start VERY small: 5 entrepreneurs per track (10 total) instead of 8-10 + 6-8 = 14-18
- Shared infrastructure: Same platform, same quarterly gatherings (different focus)
- Clear branding: "Two doors, one house" - solidarity door vs investment door
- Hire coordinator: $10K for part-time program manager to handle logistics

## RECOMMENDATION: Staged Launch

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Track 1 Solidarity Circle Pilot**
- Launch with **8 entrepreneurs** from Track 1 ranking (#1-10, prioritize email availability)
- Budget: $5,000
- Goal: Test Arabic-first governance, wellbeing protocols, co-design cooperative principles
- Success metric: 6 out of 8 complete 3 months + want to continue

**Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Reflection + Track 2 Design**
- Analyze Track 1 learnings: What governance worked? What values emerged?
- Begin Track 2 warm outreach: Contact top 10 LGA1 teams, offer value-first consultation
- Refine revenue-share model based on Track 1 governance insights
- Secure $80K funding (grant, investors, or personal capital)

**Phase 3 (Months 7-12): Track 2 Investment Pilot**
- Launch with **6 entrepreneurs** from Track 2 ranking (LGA1 teams if responsive, or drop down to ACSI2/HMN teams with websites)
- Budget: $80,000
- Goal: Deploy capital, test revenue-share mechanics, measure impact
- Success metric: 4 out of 6 successfully receive capital + make first revenue share payment

**Phase 4 (Month 13+): Integrated Model**
- Continue Track 1 as ongoing solidarity circle (self-sustaining at $10-25/month)
- Use Track 2 revenue ($7,500/year) to subsidize Track 1 ($5,000/year) + expand
- Offer "graduation pathway": Track 1 entrepreneurs who reach revenue → Track 2 invitation
- Expand both tracks based on Year 1 learnings

## Decision Criteria: Which Model Aligns with YOUR Vision?

### **Choose Track 1 First IF**:
- ✅ You want to prioritize **values alignment over financial returns**
- ✅ You want to test **Islamic cooperative principles** in practice (fitra, rizq, khilafa)
- ✅ You have **limited capital** ($5K available vs $80K)
- ✅ You want to build **trust and relationships first**, revenue later
- ✅ You want to serve **crisis-affected entrepreneurs** (Gaza, war-impacted) who need solidarity NOW
- ✅ You believe **wellbeing-centered community** is the core value proposition

### **Choose Track 2 First IF**:
- ✅ You want **financial sustainability** for the cooperative from Day 1
- ✅ You have **access to $80K capital** (grant, investors, or personal funds)
- ✅ You want to **demonstrate alternative to grants** with revenue-share model
- ✅ You prioritize **impact at scale** (larger capital deployment = larger impact)
- ✅ You want to prove the **investment cooperative** model works, then expand
- ✅ You're willing to invest time in **cold outreach** to LGA1 teams (6+ years old relationships)

### **Choose BOTH in Parallel IF**:
- ✅ You have **$85K capital** available
- ✅ You can dedicate **20+ hours/week** to program management (or hire coordinator)
- ✅ You want **maximum learning** in Year 1 (test both, iterate fast)
- ✅ You see the two tracks as **complementary** (solidarity → investment pipeline)
- ✅ You're comfortable with **complexity and ambiguity**
