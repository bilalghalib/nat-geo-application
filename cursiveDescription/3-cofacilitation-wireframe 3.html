Use Cases for Co-Facilitation:
  • Teaching teams: Multiple instructors share facilitation duties
  • TA support: Teaching assistants help with review and feedback
  • Peer facilitation: Advanced students help guide newer participants
  • Rotating leadership: Different facilitators lead different modules
  • Cross-disciplinary: Experts from different fields co-facilitate
Important: Co-facilitators have significant permissions. Only invite people you trust to help facilitate the group. They can't delete the group or change ownership, but they can send prompts and access all submissions.
